FAQs

The frequency of exercise needed to see results depends on your fitness goals and current level of fitness. The American College of Sports Medicine recommends that adults should aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity exercise or 75 minutes of vigorous-intensity exercise per week, or a combination of both. However, if your goal is to lose weight, build muscle, or improve your overall fitness, you may need to exercise more frequently and at a higher intensity. It's important to consult with a fitness professional to design a workout plan that is tailored to your specific goals and needs.

Eating the right foods before and after a workout can help improve your performance and aid in recovery. Before a workout, aim for a meal or snack that is high in carbohydrates and low in fat and fiber. Good options include a banana, oatmeal, or a protein smoothie. After a workout, focus on replenishing your energy stores and repairing muscle tissue by eating a meal or snack that is high in protein and carbohydrates. Good options include a chicken and sweet potato bowl, a protein shake, or a quinoa salad with veggies and grilled chicken.

Staying motivated can be challenging, but there are several strategies that can help. First, set realistic and achievable goals that align with your interests and passions. This will make it easier to stay committed and motivated. Second, find a workout buddy or join a fitness community that can provide support and encouragement. Third, mix up your routine to keep things interesting and challenging. Finally, celebrate your successes and be kind to yourself when you slip up. Remember that making healthy changes is a journey, not a destination, and every step counts.
